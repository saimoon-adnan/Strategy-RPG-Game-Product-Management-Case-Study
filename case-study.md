# Strategy RPG Mobile Game – Product Management Case Study

## Introduction

This case study presents a product management analysis of a **Strategy RPG mobile game**. The project was completed as part of a product management job simulation designed to demonstrate how product managers use **data-driven insights** to evaluate product performance and guide strategic decisions.

The focus of this case study is to understand player behavior, identify key performance indicators (KPIs), and determine how those KPIs can be used to solve business problems within the gaming industry.

---

# Understanding the Product

Strategy RPG (Role Playing Game) mobile games combine tactical gameplay with character progression systems. Players typically form teams, engage in turn-based battles, upgrade characters, and progress through different levels or campaigns.

## Core Game Features

- Turn-based combat system  
- Character upgrades and leveling system  
- Strategic team composition  
- In-game items and rewards  
- Limited-time events and challenges  

These mechanics are designed to keep players engaged and encourage long-term gameplay.

---

# Business Model

Most strategy RPG mobile games operate under a **Free-to-Play (F2P)** model.

This means that players can download and play the game for free while revenue is generated through:

- In-app purchases  
- Premium character unlocks  
- Special items or upgrades  
- Limited-time offers  

The success of the product depends on maintaining strong **player engagement, retention, and monetization**.

---

# Business Problem

Product managers must identify and solve business problems using product data.

Some common business questions include:

- Are players actively engaging with the game?
- Are new players returning after installing the game?
- Is the game successfully generating revenue?

To answer these questions, product managers rely on **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**.

---

# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

KPIs are measurable values used to evaluate how effectively a product is achieving its goals.

In the gaming industry, KPIs typically fall into three main categories:

## 1. Engagement Metrics

Engagement metrics measure how frequently players interact with the game.

Important engagement KPIs include:

- **Daily Active Users (DAU)**  
  Measures how many players use the game each day.

- **Monthly Active Users (MAU)**  
  Measures the total number of active players in a month.

- **Average Session Time**  
  Indicates how long players stay in the game during each session.

High engagement usually indicates that the game mechanics are enjoyable and players find value in the gameplay.

---

## 2. Retention Metrics

Retention metrics measure how well the game keeps players returning over time.

Key retention KPIs include:

- **Day 1 Retention**  
- **Day 7 Retention**  
- **Day 30 Retention**

These metrics indicate how successful the game is at maintaining long-term player interest.

Low retention may suggest problems with onboarding, difficulty balancing, or lack of engaging content.

---

## 3. Monetization Metrics

Monetization metrics evaluate how effectively the game generates revenue.

Important monetization KPIs include:

- **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)**  
- **Conversion Rate (Free users → Paying users)**  
- **Lifetime Value (LTV)**

These metrics help product managers understand the financial performance of the game.

---

# Selecting the Right KPIs

One of the most important responsibilities of a product manager is choosing the right KPIs for the right business problem.

For example:

### If the problem is low player engagement
Relevant KPIs may include:

- DAU
- Session time
- MAU

### If the problem is poor retention
Relevant KPIs may include:

- Day 1 retention
- Day 7 retention
- Day 30 retention

### If the problem is low revenue
Relevant KPIs may include:

- ARPU
- Conversion rate
- LTV

Selecting the correct KPIs ensures that product decisions are based on meaningful data.

---

# Data-Driven Decision Making

Product managers rely on analytics to transform raw data into actionable insights.

The general process includes:

1. Collecting player data  
2. Analyzing engagement and retention metrics  
3. Identifying patterns and trends  
4. Discovering performance issues  
5. Developing strategic recommendations  

This structured approach ensures that product improvements are based on evidence rather than assumptions.

---

# Key Insights

From this analysis, several important insights can be derived:

- Player engagement is critical for long-term success  
- Retention strongly impacts the lifetime value of players  
- Monetization strategies must align with player experience  

Improving these areas can significantly increase the overall performance of a mobile game.

---

# Conclusion

This case study demonstrates how product managers evaluate the performance of a mobile game using key performance indicators and data-driven analysis.

By understanding player behavior, selecting the right KPIs, and structuring insights effectively, product managers can make strategic decisions that improve both player experience and business outcomes.

This project highlights the importance of **product analytics, KPI selection, and data-driven product strategy in the gaming industry**.
